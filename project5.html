Movie Ratings Analysis

Exploratory analysis of movie ratings (IMDB-style) to find genre-level trends and guide content strategy.

Problem Statement

Producers and content platforms lacked consolidated, easy-to-explore data to understand which genres and release periods correlate with higher audience approval.

Approach

  • Cleaned and merged movies, genres and ratings tables; computed average and weighted ratings by genre and decade.
  • Built visuals to compare rating distribution and to identify top-performing genre-decade combinations.

Insights

  • Certain genres showed consistently higher median ratings (visualized in dashboard).
  • Weighted averages (by vote counts) were used to avoid bias from low-sample titles.

Business Outcome

The analysis gives content strategy teams grounding to prioritize investments — for example, focusing on genres with higher audience approval and strong vote counts, or scheduling releases in decades/anniversaries that boost discoverability. These are operational decisions (what to commission, how to market) that go beyond having segmented data in one place.
